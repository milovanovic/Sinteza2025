Slagalica - A CS Freshman Homework

  • I teach introductory programming course in Python
  • Animate students with interesting homeworks...
  • It seemd to be an ideal homework task... and it was!
  • Students were given a real dictionary in a textual file
  • Dictionary obtained from LibreOffice spell checker
  • Not the easiest homework with many side problems:
    • calculating letter probabilities in Serbian language
  • Tests: conditionals, loops, functional abstraction, I/O.
  • Surprisingly well received by the students & parents!
  • Did the same with Slovo po slovo (Hangman) game!

Moj broj - As CS Sophomore Exam

  • I tought Algorithms and Data Structures course
  • Wanted to give a bit harder problem for the exam
  • Turned out too hard: was solved only by one student
  • Since then, proved ideal as a homework task (again).
  • Example of exponential complexity algorithm O(2N):
    • however, N is small thus its runtime is reasonable
    • easy solve in postfix/reverse Polish notation (RPN)
    • exhaustive enumeration method with brute-force
    • optimizations: heuristics, memoization, pruning,...
    • reduces runtime from under 1 minute to 1 second.
  • Surprisingly well received by the students & parents!

Associations - How to approach it?

  • Apply natural language processing (NLP) techniques.
  • In particular exploit word2vec3 for word embeddings.
3Tomáš Mikolov, Ilya Sutskever, Kai Chen, Greg Corrado, Jeff Dean, "Distributed representations of words and phrases and their compositionality", NIPS'13, pp. 3111-3119

Word embedding and Word2vec

  • The word embedding approach can capture multiple different degrees of similarity between words.
  • Both semantic and syntactic patterns can be easily reproduced using plain simple vector arithmetic.
  • For example: Man is to Woman as Brother is to Sister.
4Tomáš Mikolov, Wen-tau Yih, Geoffrey Zweig, "Linguistic Regularities in Continuous Space Word Representations", NAACL HLT 2013, pp. 746-751

Word2vec applied to the Associations

5Nebojša Grujić, Vladimir Milovanović, "Associative Word Relations in Natural Language Processing", Applied Artificial Intelligence, vol. 36, no. 1, article: 2034262, February 2022

How to obtain The Word Embeddings?

  • Training corpus was Serbian only and it included6:
    1. Serbian Wikipedia and Matica srpska dictionaries
    2. OCR-ed Serbian literature from National Library
    3. Crawling snapshot of the .rs top-level domain
  • After the recurrent neural network (RNN) language model is trained, the learned word embeddings are positioned in the vector space such that words that share common contexts in the corpus — that is, words that are semantically and syntactically similar — are located close to one another in the space.
6Nebojša Grujić, Vladimir Milovanović, "Natural Language Processing for Associative Word Predictions", IEEE EUROCON 2019 - International Conference on Smart Technologies, Novi Sad, Serbia, July 2019

All Starts with Prompt Engineering

  • Turns out the following prompt works effectively:
    • We speak in Serbian language. We are playing an association game where I give four words and You have to find the fifth word that connects them. It can be used correctly in a linguistic context with all four words, without the concepts represented by these expressions being necessarily connected. I already know it, and it is not one of the mentioned words. List ten words that could be the correct answer, in order of decreasing probability, separated by commas, without numbering.

Future Work and Conclusions

  • Latest LLMs reached human-level performance in Associations game with all 4 clue words uncovered.
  • General performance still remains to be evaluated.
  • Room for improvement exists, apart from GPT-4.5:
    • AI-assisted optimal prompt engineering & divising.
    • Overall joint performance in all 7 Slagalica games.
  • Semantic association capability is transformer architecture core mechanism.
  • Seems that the game of Associations in particular, and Game Shows in general can be useful banchmark for assesing LLMs, and it's fun afterall!
